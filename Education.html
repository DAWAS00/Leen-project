Understanding Childhood Trauma

Knowledge is the first step towards healing

Recognizing Trauma Symptoms

  • Increased anxiety and fear
  • Sleep disturbances and nightmares
  • Startled by loud noises
  • Changes in behavior or mood
  • Physical symptoms (headaches, stomach aches)

Creating Safe Environments

  • Establish consistent daily routines
  • Create quiet, comfortable spaces
  • Maintain open communication
  • Provide comfort items (blankets, toys)
  • Ensure emotional support is available

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I help my child feel safe?

Maintain routines, provide consistent support, and create a calm environment. Regular reassurance and open communication are essential.

When should I seek professional help?

If symptoms persist or interfere with daily activities, consider consulting a mental health professional who specializes in childhood trauma.

How can I explain trauma to children?

Use age-appropriate language and simple explanations. Focus on safety and healing rather than traumatic details.

For immediate support, please contact mental health professionals or emergency services.
